Steve Jobs

Was an American business magnate, industrial designer, investor, and media proprietor. He was the chairman, chief executive officer, and co-founder of Apple Inc. as well as the chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar.

Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak notes that Jobs never learned how to code.
Microcomputers
Apple

Jobs and Steve Wozniak co-founded Apple in 1976 to sell Wozniak's Apple I personal computer. Together the duo gained fame and wealth a year later with the Apple II, one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers. This led to the development of the breakthrough Macintosh in 1984, the first mass-produced computer with a GUI.
